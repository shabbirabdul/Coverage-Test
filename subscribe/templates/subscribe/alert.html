Outlook Highlights

  • Drought removal is anticipated across much of southern Mississippi, Alabama, and the Florida Panhandle
  • Drought persistence is anticipated across southwestern Mississippi and Louisiana 
  • Drought expansion across the central Gulf Coast is due to below median precipitation, along with decreasing soil moisture and low streamflow, has promoted drought expansion
  • Additional drought reduction across the Tennessee Valley is predicted as a result of widespread precipitation

Outlook Summary

This alert is derived from the US Monthly Drought Outlook dated insert monthly date from NOAA's Climate Prediction Center. The Outlook includes broad-scale trends based on subjectively derived probabilities and guided by short- and long-range statistical and dynamical forecasts. Changes in drought areas are related to US Drought Monitor intensity categories. Areas of ongoing drought are approximated from category D1 to D4. Drought improvement implies that an area will experience at least a one-category improvement by the end of the Outlook period. Drought removal implies that an area will reach D0 by the end of the Outlook period (source).

Fast Fact: Across the region, 203,897 square miles are impacted by the monthly drought alert, and 117,050 (or 57%) of those were also impacted this time last year.
